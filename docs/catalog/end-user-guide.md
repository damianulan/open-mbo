# End User Guide

## Overview

OpenMBO supports goal management inside an organization. The platform combines campaign-based performance processes with individual objective tracking, evaluations, and reward points.

Depending on your role, you can use OpenMBO as:

- an employee who completes assigned objectives,
- a supervisor who reviews progress,
- a coordinator who manages campaigns and assignments,
- an HR or admin user who maintains people and organization data,
- a system administrator who configures modules and application settings.

The exact menu items visible in your account depend on your permissions.

## What you can do in OpenMBO

### 1. Use the dashboard as your starting point

After signing in, the dashboard gives you a quick summary of your work in the system.

From here you can:

- see your active campaigns,
- review your current objectives,
- jump directly to the most important tasks,
- monitor your recent progress at a glance.

### 2. Work with your objectives

The **My objectives** area is the main workspace for regular users.

Here you can:

- see all objectives assigned to you,
- check objective status, deadline, category, and related campaign,
- open objective details,
- review realization and evaluation values,
- track points awarded for completed work,
- see which objectives are still active and which are already completed.

Objective statuses help you understand what is happening with each task. Depending on the process stage, an objective may appear as:

- unstarted,
- in progress,
- completed,
- passed,
- failed,
- interrupted.

If an objective belongs to a campaign, its status can change automatically as the campaign moves through its lifecycle.

### 3. Follow campaigns

Campaigns are structured performance cycles that group objectives and participants into one process.

Inside a campaign you can:

- read the campaign description,
- see the campaign period and current stage,
- review the timeline of campaign phases,
- browse campaign objectives,
- see enrolled participants,
- understand whether the campaign is active, completed, terminated, canceled, or managed manually.

Campaigns use soft stages and hard statuses.

Soft stages describe the working phase of the process:

- definition,
- disposition,
- realization,
- evaluation,
- self-evaluation.

Hard statuses describe the overall state of the campaign:

- pending,
- in progress,
- completed,
- terminated,
- canceled.

For regular participants, campaigns are mainly a way to understand context and timing. For coordinators and admins, campaigns are also the place where users and objectives are managed.

### 4. Monitor your points

OpenMBO can assign reward points to campaign participation and objective completion.

You can use the points area to:

- see your total points balance,
- review recently granted points,
- open the item that generated the points,
- understand how your activity is being rewarded.

### 5. Read and manage notifications

The notifications module stores system messages related to your activity.

You can:

- open the notifications list,
- identify unread messages,
- read the full content of a selected notification,
- use notifications as an entry point to relevant workflow items.

Notifications help you stay aware of assignments, updates, and other important system events.

### 6. Manage your profile and preferences

Every user can manage their own account information.

In the profile area you can:

- update your first name, last name, and email address,
- upload or change your avatar,
- maintain personal profile details such as birthday,
- review your own recorded activity in the system.

In preferences you can:

- choose language and theme preferences,
- control mail notifications,
- control in-app notifications,
- enable or disable extended and system notifications.

### 7. View people in the organization

If your role allows it, the **Users** area lets you work with employee records.

Depending on permissions, you may be able to:

- browse the user list,
- open user profiles,
- see organization details such as company, department, position, and contract type,
- add users to favourites,
- edit user data,
- manage employments,
- reset passwords,
- block or unblock accounts,
- impersonate a user for support or troubleshooting,
- delete accounts.

Some users may only have preview access instead of full profile access.

### 8. Manage objective templates and categories

Users with management permissions can work with reusable objective structures.

This part of the system allows you to:

- create and edit objective templates,
- organize templates into categories,
- assign category or objective administrators,
- reuse standardized objective definitions in campaigns,
- keep goal planning consistent across teams.

Templates are useful when the same types of objectives appear repeatedly in different campaigns or departments.

### 9. Manage objectives beyond your own account

Coordinators, supervisors, and other authorized users can manage broader objective flows.

Depending on access rights, you may be able to:

- create objectives,
- edit objective content,
- assign objectives to users,
- evaluate realization,
- mark an objective as passed or failed,
- review objective completion across many users,
- add notes and comments related to the objective.

This makes OpenMBO suitable not only for self-service tracking, but also for formal review processes.

### 10. Manage campaigns and participants

Campaign coordinators and administrators can use campaign pages as an operational workspace.

Available actions may include:

- creating campaigns,
- editing campaign details and timeline,
- assigning campaign coordinators,
- enrolling or removing users,
- adding campaign objectives,
- switching campaigns between active and inactive states,
- terminating, resuming, or canceling campaigns,
- enabling manual stage handling for specific participant records.

Manual mode is useful when a participant should not follow the default automatic stage progression.

### 11. Maintain organizational structure

Administrative users can keep the business structure up to date.

This includes:

- companies,
- departments,
- positions,
- teams,
- contract types.

These records are used throughout the application to organize people and reporting context.

### 12. Configure the application

Some modules are available only to administrators.

Administrative configuration areas include:

- general application settings,
- module management,
- notification definitions,
- server and mail settings,
- logs,
- branding and visual identity settings.

These sections are intended for operational maintenance rather than day-to-day employee usage.

## Typical user journeys

### Employee

An employee usually uses OpenMBO to:

- open the dashboard,
- review active campaigns,
- check assigned objectives,
- update realization data when required,
- monitor deadlines, evaluations, and points,
- read notifications.

### Supervisor or evaluator

A supervisor usually uses OpenMBO to:

- review assigned users,
- inspect progress on objectives,
- evaluate realization,
- mark objectives as passed or failed,
- follow campaign progress.

### Coordinator

A coordinator usually uses OpenMBO to:

- create or update campaigns,
- assign participants,
- attach objectives to campaigns,
- monitor campaign stages,
- correct manual stage flow when necessary.

### Administrator

An administrator usually uses OpenMBO to:

- manage users and employment data,
- maintain organizational structure,
- configure modules and settings,
- manage notification templates,
- inspect logs and server settings.

## Access model

OpenMBO does not expose the same interface to every user.

What you can do depends on:

- your global role,
- campaign-specific coordinator assignments,
- objective or category permissions,
- module availability in the system configuration.

If you cannot see a page or action described in this guide, the most likely reason is that your account does not currently have the required permission.

## Summary

OpenMBO is a role-based application for managing goals, campaigns, evaluations, and organizational structure.

At a basic level, most users will use it to:

- follow campaigns,
- complete objectives,
- review statuses and deadlines,
- collect points,
- read notifications,
- manage their own profile.

Advanced users can additionally use it to:

- manage people,
- configure campaign workflows,
- assign and evaluate objectives,
- maintain structure and settings for the whole organization.
